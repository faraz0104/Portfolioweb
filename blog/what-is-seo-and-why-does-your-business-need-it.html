What is SEO and Why Does Your Business Need It?

93% of online experiences begin with a search engine. If your business isn't showing up on Google's first page, you're invisible to thousands of potential customers every day.

F
Faraz Khan
SEO Expert & Web Developer
📅 June 1, 2025
⏱ 7 min read

Imagine you open a shop in a busy market, but the shop is hidden in a dark alley with no sign. That's what having a website without SEO is like. SEO (Search Engine Optimisation) is the process of making your website visible to people searching for your products or services on Google.

What Exactly is SEO?

SEO stands for Search Engine Optimisation. It's the practice of optimising your website so it appears higher in Google search results when people search for terms related to your business.

For example, if you're a web developer in Mumbai and someone searches "web developer in Mumbai", SEO helps your website appear in the top results — before your competitors.

The higher you rank, the more people click your website, and the more enquiries and sales you get. It's that simple.

How Do Search Engines Work?

Google uses automated programs called "crawlers" (or spiders) that constantly browse the web, reading every page they can find. When they find your website, they:

  1. Crawl — read all your pages and follow links
  2. Index — store your content in Google's massive database
  3. Rank — decide where you appear for specific searches based on 200+ factors

Your job (with SEO) is to make your website as easy to crawl, index, and rank as possible.

The 3 Types of SEO

1. On-Page SEO

Optimising what's on your actual pages:

  • Keyword-rich title tags and meta descriptions
  • Proper heading structure (H1 → H2 → H3)
  • High-quality, relevant content
  • Image alt tags and compressed images
  • Internal linking between your pages

2. Technical SEO

Making your website fast and easy for Google to crawl:

  • Page speed optimisation (aim for under 3 seconds)
  • Mobile-friendly design
  • XML sitemap and robots.txt
  • HTTPS / SSL certificate
  • Schema markup (structured data)
  • Fixing broken links and crawl errors

3. Off-Page SEO (Link Building)

Building your website's authority through external signals:

  • Backlinks from other reputable websites
  • Google Business Profile (for local businesses)
  • Social media mentions and shares
  • Business directory listings (Justdial, Sulekha, Clutch)

Why SEO is Especially Important for Businesses in India

India has over 900 million internet users — and that number is growing every year. Consider these facts:

  • Over 70% of Indian consumers research online before making a purchase
  • Google processes over 8.5 billion searches per day worldwide
  • 75% of users never scroll past the first page of Google results
  • The top 3 organic results get over 50% of all clicks
"The best place to hide a dead body is page 2 of Google search results." — Digital marketing saying

With most Indian businesses still not investing in SEO, the opportunity to dominate your local and national search results has never been better.

Local SEO for Indian Businesses

If you serve customers in a specific city — say, Delhi, Mumbai, Bangalore, or Hyderabad — Local SEO is your most powerful tool. It helps you appear in:

  • Google Maps results ("web developer near me")
  • The "Local Pack" — the 3 business listings shown at the top of local searches
  • City-specific search results ("web developer in Pune")

Setting up and optimising your Google Business Profile (formerly Google My Business) is free and can drive significant footfall and enquiries.

What ROI Can You Expect from SEO?

One of my clients — a home renovation contractor — was spending ₹20,000/month on Google Ads with moderate results. After a 4-month SEO campaign, they ranked in the top 3 for 20 local keywords and now get 60+ leads per month from organic search — at zero ongoing ad cost.

SEO typically delivers 5–10x ROI over a 12-month period compared to paid advertising, especially in India where competition is still lower than in Western markets.

Getting Started with SEO

The best time to start SEO was when you launched your website. The second best time is right now. Here's how to begin:

  1. Set up Google Search Console (free) and submit your sitemap
  2. Create or optimise your Google Business Profile
  3. Research 10–20 keywords your customers search for
  4. Optimise your pages with those keywords naturally
  5. Build backlinks from directories and partner sites
  6. Start a blog — this article is a perfect example of SEO content
